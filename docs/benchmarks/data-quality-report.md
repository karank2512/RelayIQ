# Data-quality report (synthetic benchmark, 2026-07-11)

All numbers below are **measured on seeded synthetic data** (seed 42, 200 companies,
510 contacts, 15% duplicate submissions) with simulated providers and known ground truth.
They are regenerated by `make benchmark` / `make calibration`; formulas for every metric are
in [metric-definitions.md](metric-definitions.md). None of these are claims about live
commercial providers.

## Field-level quality vs reviewed (synthetic) truth

| Strategy | Fill rate (eligible) | Field precision | Review load |
|---|---|---|---|
| Naive both-providers | 0.9221 | 0.6820 | — |
| Static field routing + reconciliation | 0.9317 | 0.7730 | 0 |
| Full RelayIQ (cross-check + confidence gate) | 0.9240 | **0.7836** | ~5% of records |

Key mechanism: the two simulated providers disagree most on `job_title` (Provider Alpha serves
stale titles for job-changers). Field routing sends people-fields to the fresh provider;
reconciliation groups value-equivalent answers ("VP Sales" ≡ "Vice President of Sales") and
escalates true conflicts ("VP Sales" vs "Director of Sales") to review instead of guessing.

## Confidence calibration (rules-v1)

- **Brier score:** 0.1642 · **ECE:** 0.0905 · n = 783 scored fields
- The score is **overconfident above 0.8**: the 0.9–1.0 bucket (n=428) shows ~80% accuracy.
- Interpretation shipped with the report: treat rules-v1 as a **ranking signal** for review
  prioritization and gating, not a probability. The planned learned model (roadmap) must beat
  this baseline on ECE before deployment (ADR-005).

Full reliability table + diagram: [calibration.md](calibration.md).

## Known measurement caveats

- "Precision vs truth" uses `values_equivalent` (normalized comparison) — stricter than
  human review for free-text fields, looser for numeric ranges (±15% employee-count tolerance).
- Review-queue records are excluded from the full pipeline's usable-lead count (human review
  is not simulated); its cost-per-usable is therefore conservative.
- Wrong-account and false-merge rates are represented in the synthetic world
  (`wrong_account_in_provider`, `duplicate_of` tags) but the current benchmark scores field
  values, not entity-resolution errors — an explicit gap noted in the roadmap.
